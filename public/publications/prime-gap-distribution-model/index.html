Modeling Local Irregularities in Prime Gap Distribution Under 10^7

PublishedJune 20, 2025
AuthorYour Name

Abstract

An exploratory computational study of prime gap size distribution below ten million, testing how well a simple probabilistic model predicts observed local clustering.

Research question

How well does a Poisson-based model of prime gaps predict the observed frequency of unusually large and unusually small gaps below 10^7?

Methodology

Primes below 10^7 were generated with a sieve algorithm; gap sizes were computed and binned, then compared against the distribution predicted by the Cramér model using a chi-squared goodness-of-fit test.

Key findings

The Cramér model closely matched observed gap frequency for mid-range gaps but underestimated the frequency of the largest observed gaps, consistent with known limitations of the model at scale.

Significance

Provides a hands-on, code-verified illustration of a known limitation in a classical number-theoretic model, and demonstrates applied computational methods for independent mathematical research.
