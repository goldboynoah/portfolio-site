Local Minimum Wage Increases and Teen Employment: A County-Level Comparison

PublishedNovember 2, 2025
AuthorYour Name
CategoryEconomics

Abstract

A comparative analysis of teen employment rates in neighboring counties with differing minimum wage policies, using publicly available labor statistics to examine short-run employment effects.

Research question

Do county-level minimum wage increases correspond to measurable short-run changes in teen employment relative to neighboring counties without the increase?

Methodology

Employment data for 16-19 year-olds was collected for treatment and comparison counties over eight quarters surrounding a policy change, using a difference-in-differences approach to isolate the policy effect from broader regional trends.

Key findings

The analysis found a small, not statistically significant decline in teen employment in treatment counties relative to comparison counties, suggesting limited short-run disemployment effects at the wage levels studied.

Significance

Contributes a small, transparent, replicable data point to the broader minimum wage literature, and demonstrates applied difference-in-differences methodology using public data sources.
